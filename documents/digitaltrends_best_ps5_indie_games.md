# The Best Indie Games on PS5 — Digital Trends

Source: https://www.digitaltrends.com/gaming/best-ps5-indie-games/
Type: Editorial genre ranking (Indie)
Domain: PS5 Game Discovery Guide
Note: Digital Trends' picks for the best indie games on PS5 (by Jesse Lennox). Percentages are aggregate review scores. Indie spans many genres — Metroidvania, roguelike, RPG, sim, puzzle, horror.

---

## Stardew Valley — 87% — RPG / Simulation / Adventure (2016, ConcernedApe)
A small-town life sim: inherit a run-down farm and live day to day, choosing priorities — growing crops, helping townsfolk, exploring caves. Dozens of hours of enjoyment, extendable with mods. A relaxing, open-ended favorite.

## Balatro — 87% — Strategy / Card / Roguelike (2024, LocalThunk / Playstack)
A poker roguelike from a single developer that captured the whole industry. Easy to start if you know basic poker hands; the joker system evolves in clearly communicated ways. Play for five minutes or five hours and have a blast either way.

## Haven — 74% — RPG / Adventure / Dating Sim (2020, The Game Bakers)
A cross between an RPG and a dating sim following two lovers, Yu and Kay, surviving a hostile world. Split between developing their (already established) relationship, turn-based combat, and exploration. Inventive, writing-driven — a prime example of indie creativity. **Two-character / couch-friendly.**

## Bugsnax — 77% — Puzzle / Adventure (2020, Young Horses)
A charming, quirky first-person adventure — a cross between Viva Piñata and Cloudy With a Chance of Meatballs, with a Pokémon Snap element. Not challenging; colorful and laid-back with surprisingly sharp writing. **Great for kids and casual players.**

## Crow Country — 83% — Survival Horror / Puzzle / Adventure (2024, SFB Games)
Feels like a lost PS1-era Resident Evil in all the best ways: manage resources, slowly explore an old amusement park that opens up as you solve puzzles, with a sinister plot and off-kilter characters. Tight, terrifying retro survival horror. **Indie horror pick.**

## No Man's Sky — 69% — Shooter / RPG / Simulation / Adventure (2016, Hello Games)
A famously rocky launch turned around by years of updates: revamped visuals, real multiplayer, better building/customization. An endless, colorful space-exploration game with meaningfully varied worlds. "Finally the game it was meant to be." **Supports multiplayer.**

## Kena: Bridge of Spirits — 79% — Action / RPG / Adventure (2021, Ember Lab)
Stunning graphics that belie its indie origins (Ember Lab's first game). Feels like a modern PS2 action platformer: fun platforming, light puzzle-solving, and surprisingly tough battles as Kena, a Spirit Guide. **Family-friendly action-adventure.**

## Untitled Goose Game — 79% — Puzzle / Simulation / Adventure (2019, House House / Panic)
Play a goose terrorizing an English village — steal items, destroy property, and honk. Peaceful minimalist art and calm soundtrack. **Two-player co-op** in the latest update ("two geese are better than one"). A sleeper hit, great with a friend.

## Animal Well — 79% — Platformer / Puzzle / Metroidvania (2024, Shared Memory / Bigmode)
The work of one passionate developer — a remarkably open, off-beat 2D Metroidvania. Follow your curiosity to find alternate uses for strange powers to fight and solve puzzles. Best experienced blind, for the satisfaction when things click.

## Disco Elysium: The Final Cut — 95% — RPG / Adventure (2021, ZA/UM)
"The most ambitious game maybe ever." A detective story so strong in narrative and writing that it has no combat at all — speech checks and physical actions resolve via stats and dice rolls, and failing a roll adapts the story rather than ending it. "A masterpiece every gamer should experience."

## Hades — 93% — RPG / Hack-and-slash / Roguelike (2020, Supergiant Games)
The roguelike benchmark: huge variety between weapons, powers, rooms, and upgrades, plus the best-feeling progression in the genre — each run moves you forward mechanically or narratively, wrapped in a beautiful art style.

## Outer Wilds — 91% — Puzzle / Adventure (2019, Mobius Digital / Annapurna)
An open exploration-and-puzzle game where you go wherever you want to solve a big mystery (best unspoiled). The puzzles require genuine thought and figuring out solutions yourself, making it uniquely satisfying. (Not to be confused with The Outer Worlds.)

## Chicory: A Colorful Tale — 80% — Puzzle / RPG / Adventure (2021, Greg Lobanov / Finji)
A lightly Zelda-inspired game about a cute dog bringing color back to a blank coloring-book world. Solve puzzles, meet charismatic NPCs, and color in as much as you like. A complete zen experience with no pressure — relaxing for all ages.
