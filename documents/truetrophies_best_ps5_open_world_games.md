# Best Open-World Games on PS5 — Top 25 Ranked (TrueTrophies)

Source: https://www.truetrophies.com/news/best-open-world-games-playstation
Type: Editorial genre ranking (Open-World)
Domain: PS5 Game Discovery Guide
Note: TrueTrophies' top 25 open-world games playable on PS5 (native, backward-compatible, or via PS Plus). Includes "Is it on PS Plus?" for each — useful for subscription-based discovery.

---

## 1. Red Dead Redemption 2 — Rockstar North (2018) — PS Plus: No
The best open-world game on PS5. Cowboy Arthur Morgan in a dying Old West; revolutionary environment design where you can interact with everyone and stumble into emergent random events. Possibly the best story in gaming, elevated by how it interplays with the world. "Nothing you do feels arbitrary."

## 2. Kingdom Come: Deliverance II — Warhorse Studios (2025) — PS Plus: No
The outstanding RPG of the PS5 generation. An outstanding two-part recreation of medieval Bohemia where every part of the land feels purposefully used, with a fully functioning society, earnest writing, and remarkable characters.

## 3. The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt — CD Projekt Red (2015 / 2022 PS5) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
A grounded high-fantasy open world rooted in relatable landscapes (Skellige cliffs, Crookback bogs, Oxenfurt). Superb writing, complex creatures, emotional character arcs, and Eastern-European humor. "Embarrasses every other RPG of the last 10 years." New PS5 platinum.

## 4. Elden Ring — FromSoftware (2022) — PS Plus: No
Open-world Soulslike with organic boss fights naturally integrated into a huge, diverse world (and a double-jumping horse). Amplifies Dark Souls' risk/reward exploration; tackle it in any direction. "The ideal water-cooler open-world game" — everyone comes away with their own story.

## 5. Marvel's Spider-Man 2 — Insomniac Games (2023) — PS Plus: No
Its New York exists as a giant obstacle course expressing Spider-Man's movement creatively. Wind tunnels, vents, Super Slingshots, and Web Wings turn getting from A to B into a skill. Easy platinum.

## 6. Grand Theft Auto V — Rockstar North (2013 / 2022 PS5) — PS Plus: No
Even 10+ years on, Los Santos remains one of the most vibrant contemporary open worlds, satirizing American hyper-capitalism. Satisfying driving, good gunplay, a brilliant story, plus the decent GTA Online mode.

## 7. Cyberpunk 2077 — CD Projekt Red (2020 / 2022 PS5) — PS Plus: No
After a rocky start, Night City is now a deliriously vibrant dystopian metropolis full of content — taking down Cyberpsychos, parkouring with cyber enhancements, and engaging side quests. The Phantom Liberty expansion is excellent.

## 8. Ghost of Tsushima — Sucker Punch (2020 / 2021 PS5) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
The traditional open-world formula nailed, with Feudal Japan twists: a landscape that feels fresh to Western audiences and a guiding-wind system that reduces menu clutter, making the world larger and more immersive. Fantastic combat and a strong narrative.

## 9. Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth — Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio (2024) — PS Plus: No
Set in Hawaii with refined turn-based combat; a gripping story and an insane side-content feast. Returning Japanese settings make it the most complete open world of the Yakuza games. A surprisingly easy, enjoyable platinum.

## 10. Horizon Forbidden West — Guerrilla Games (2022) — PS Plus: No
"The most technically beautiful game" on PS5 — decrepit sand-swallowed cities, snowy hill villages, stunning lighting, and a reactive Aloy. Refines the Ubisoft-style open world to a tee, with an excellent combat model featuring giant robot dinosaurs.

## 11. The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim — Bethesda (2011 / 2021 PS5) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
The archetypal massive, immersive open world. Endless content — side quests, secrets, enemy-filled caves — that rewards ignoring the main story for dozens of hours. Still holds up.

## 12. Dragon's Dogma 2 — Capcom (2024) — PS Plus: No
A huge world filled with mysteries and nightmarish terrors (a riddling sphinx, a petrifying Medusa). More focus on exploration than main story; delving into caves and scouring for treasure is where the fun lives.

## 13. Final Fantasy VII Rebirth — Square Enix (2024) — PS Plus: No
Doles out its world in stunning chunks, pacing a solid story alongside hours of side content. A win-win for story-focused players and those who like to get lost.

## 14. Death Stranding — Kojima Productions (2019 / 2021 PS5) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
Play Sam Porter Bridges delivering parcels to reconnect a broken America. Voyages over mountains, chasms, snow, and acidic rain with bikes, trucks, grapples, and cannons. Gorgeous and unique — "the environment is the game." Not for everyone, but worth finding out.

## 15. No Man's Sky — Hello Games (2016 / 2020 PS5) — PS Plus: No
Regularly updated into a far richer game; the Worlds Part 2 update added another universe. Repair your ship, then explore never-before-seen planets — reaching the most inhospitable worlds is its own magical reward. **Supports multiplayer.**

## 16. Star Wars Outlaws — Massive Entertainment (2024) — PS Plus: No
Reports of poor quality are exaggerated — combat/stealth are middling but world-building and characters shine, with lovingly crafted planets and thrilling set-pieces. Could make you a Star Wars fan.

## 17. Hogwarts Legacy — Avalanche Games (2023) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
A standout recreation of Hogwarts bursting with Harry Potter's magic systems — twisting corridors, towers, brilliant music, and the Scottish Highlands beyond. Strong gameplay and fun side missions, even if the main story wanders.

## 18. Sea of Thieves — Rare (2024 PS5) — PS Plus: No
A great pirate game capturing pop-culture pirate iconography — green islands, translucent seas, sunny skies. Half the appeal is the environment. **Grab a crew (co-op multiplayer).**

## 19. Assassin's Creed Shadows — Ubisoft Quebec (2025) — PS Plus: No
A pleasant surprise — a dense but not bloated feudal Japan that nudges rather than shoves you through objectives, allowing organic distraction. "The most fun in an AC world since Black Flag."

## 20. Stellar Blade — Shift Up (2024) — PS Plus: No
Hack-and-slash with an open world split into manageable, distinct sections — restraint that keeps each area fun and fresh to explore for collectibles, side missions, and secrets.

## 21. Infinity Nikki — Papergames/InFold (2024) — PS Plus: No (free-to-play)
"The coziest open-world game you'll ever play." Explore Miraland as Stylist Nikki, collecting to craft outfits and playing minigames. Optional gacha; the best loop is just cozy collecting.

## 22. Days Gone — Bend Studio (2019) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
Underrated PS4-era game: biker Deacon St. John shooting "freakers" (zombies) across a gloriously open, wild Oregon. A compelling, meaty gameplay loop — especially when a huge horde chases you.

## 23. GhostWire: Tokyo — Tango Gameworks (2022) — PS Plus: No
A haunting, abandoned, neon-lit Tokyo to wander, exploring freaky Japanese mythology with weird, unsettling vibes. The open world doesn't push boundaries but the atmosphere delivers.

## 24. Atomic Heart — Mundfish (2023) — PS Plus: No
A fantastic open-world FPS set in an alternate-history 1950s Soviet Union — very BioShock-like, with wrist-mounted abilities, malfunctioning robots, loot, bosses, and collectibles.

## 25. Forspoken — Luminous Productions (2023) — PS Plus: Yes (Extra)
A maligned but abundantly generous open world where movement and action feel incredible (gliding, spell-casting). Dialogue is widely criticized — a quick fix is switching voice to Japanese. A PS5 exclusive.
