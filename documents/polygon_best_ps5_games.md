# The 25 Best PlayStation 5 Games — Polygon

Source: https://www.polygon.com/best-ps5-games-playstation-5/
Type: Editorial ranking (living list, Polygon staff picks)
Domain: PS5 Game Discovery Guide
Note: A curated list chosen for quality plus breadth and variety across genres. Unordered (not a strict ranking). Each entry is personally recommended by at least one Polygon staffer.

---

## Pragmata — Capcom — Sci-Fi Action / Third-Person Shooter
A cinematically ambitious sci-fi action game about fatherhood and the AI threat: a man in a spacesuit shooting robots on a moon base, managing resources between save points. Your childlike android companion Diana adds a grid-based hacking minigame that blends compelling quickfire puzzles into the shooting. Plays solidly, looks sharp, and feels fresh.

## Alan Wake 2 — Remedy Entertainment — Survival Horror
A survival-horror masterpiece and meta-mystery. Horror author Alan is joined by FBI agent Saga Anderson; you can fluidly switch between their stories. Twists from methodical detective thriller to reality-bending cosmic horror. "Announces the start of a new generation of blockbuster horror gaming."

## Animal Well — Billy Basso — Puzzle / Metroidvania
A strange, spooky puzzle adventure compared to indie classic Fez. You play an egg-blob-thing in a spectral forest of ghostly animals, building a 2D platforming map non-linearly. Best played with a notebook in hand — a giant puzzle written in a language you learn as you go. Gorgeous translucent pixel art.

## Astro Bot — Team Asobi — Platformer (PS5 exclusive)
"Surely the best platformer Sony has ever released" — stands toe-to-toe with Nintendo's greatest. A stunning technical showcase of the DualSense, a nonstop riot of invention, and a moving celebration of PlayStation history with hundreds of collectible bots dressed as PlayStation characters.

## Baldur's Gate 3 — Larian Studios — RPG
A hardcore Dungeons & Dragons-based RPG that became a massive hit. As close to tabletop roleplaying as video games get: strong storytelling, indelible characters, incredible flexibility and player agency. The PS5 version offers split-screen co-op. "One of the best role-playing games of all time."

## Blue Prince — Dogubomb — Puzzle / Roguelike
An extraordinary combination of walking sim, puzzle game, roguelike, and deck-builder about exploring an ever-shifting mansion. Each door you try, you pick the next room from three random blueprints. A bewitching game of chance, mystery, and copious note-taking. A true original.

## Clair Obscur: Expedition 33 — Sandfall Interactive — Turn-Based RPG
For fans nostalgic for the Final Fantasy 13 era: lush modern visuals with old-school turn-based battles, "with added Frenchness." A bewitching, deeply rewarding RPG with an excellent combat system, endearing characters, imaginative art, and a moving storyline. A true original that won't waste your time.

## Cyberpunk 2077: Ultimate Edition — CD Projekt Red — Open-World FPS-RPG
A Herculean turnaround from its ignominious launch into a definitive modern FPS-RPG. The native PS5 version plus the Phantom Liberty expansion delivered sweeping upgrades and a compelling new storyline. A 1980s-inflected cyberpunk fantasia mixing Deus Ex, GTA, Blade Runner, and The Matrix — and you're Keanu Reeves' best friend.

## Death Stranding 2: On the Beach — Kojima Productions — Action (traversal)
Hideo Kojima's quixotic epic returns — strange and lavish, but easier to get along with. More tools early, eased stealth and combat, more gameplay variety, and more character-focused storytelling. The painstaking gameplay of route planning, cargo loading, and hiking is as weirdly compelling as before.

## Despelote — Julián Cordero & Sebastian Valbuena — Narrative / Art Game
"Something completely different" — a first-person act of memory and documentary about a kid in Ecuador watching the national soccer team's 2002 World Cup qualifying run. Expressive 2D line-drawn characters over grainy 3D environments, with sophisticated first-person soccer controls. A unique, sophisticated artwork.

## Disco Elysium: The Final Cut — ZA/UM — RPG
A grungy detective RPG about politics, alcoholism, and failure. Only Baldur's Gate 3 rivals its freedom in how you make choices and define your character. Little turn-based combat; instead a deep skill system (ideas like feminism are stat buffs) and tortured internal dialogues. Utterly idiosyncratic — there's nothing else like it.

## Elden Ring — FromSoftware — Open-World Action-RPG (Soulslike)
FromSoftware's demanding action-RPG format blown out into an epic open world, gaining scale, freedom, and discovery while keeping its mysterious, sorrowful soul. Still challenging, but patience and planning take you far. The openness and flexibility of its class designs make it From's most inviting game. A modern classic.

## Final Fantasy 14 — Square Enix — MMORPG
The only MMORPG to truly emerge from World of Warcraft's shadow. After a disastrous 2010 launch, producer Naoki Yoshida relaunched it in 2013; it's now an all-time titan of the genre. A beautiful world with strong storylines, flexible character classes, fun PvE dungeons/raids, a solo "story mode," and it plays beautifully on console.

## Forza Horizon 5 — Playground Games — Open-World Racing
A former Xbox exclusive arriving on PS5 — the best, most accessible, most purely enjoyable racing series for over a decade. A spectacular open-world Mexico, upbeat festival vibe, a thoughtfully curated car list, slick graphics, entertaining physics, and a mountain of on/off-road racing and chill exploring.

## Ghost of Yōtei — Sucker Punch — Open-World Action-Adventure
Sequel to Ghost of Tsushima — stunningly beautiful in tech and art direction, set in 1600s Ezo, Japan. A showcase of elegant open-world design that guides and rewards exploration, with finely balanced, impactful combat. "Such a well-made game" — the late-stage pinnacle of this kind of design.

## God of War Ragnarök — SIE Santa Monica Studio — Action-Adventure
A grandstanding cinematic beat-'em-up: a well-balanced action game with satisfying RPG-lite advancement, a smart script, and a lovable family around gruff hero Kratos (with Richard Schiff as Odin). Largely on-rails spectacle full of delightful button-mashing and Big Moments. "An extremely solid blockbuster."

## Gran Turismo 7 — Polyphony Digital — Racing Sim
"A PlayStation console just doesn't feel complete without its Gran Turismo." One of the most satisfying, accessible, fun entries in the series. Excellent driving simulation and peerless photorealistic visuals; the charming single-player Café mode guides you through a thoughtfully selected garage. "Still the thinking person's racing series."

## Helldivers 2 — Arrowhead Game Studios — Co-op Third-Person Shooter
"What if Starship Troopers was a co-operative third-person shooter?" A perfect, no-frills online game for a squad of pals: tactical, lethal, surprising, tense, and funny, garnished with unsubtle satire. A massively multiplayer meta-layer has the whole player base unlock challenges together. "What live-service games should be like."

## Like a Dragon: Infinite Wealth — Ryu Ga Gotoku Studio — Turn-Based RPG
The most ambitious, surreal, emotional, hilarious, and huge game in the Yakuza/Like a Dragon series. Once past hours of backstory, an astonishing playground opens for Ichiban and Kiryu in Hawaii: deep, silly turn-based combat, hours of drama, and dozens of minigames (knockoffs of Pokémon, Animal Crossing, Crazy Taxi).

## Metaphor: ReFantazio — Studio Zero / Atlus — Fantasy JRPG
A triumphant application of everything that makes Persona great to a riveting new fantasy world. Crisp, fast-paced turn-based combat, complex relationship affinities, and a calendar-based structure. Grand world-building in an anime-flavored fantasy where forces vie for power and "Human" monsters terrorize the land. A classic RPG epic brought up to date.

## Resident Evil Requiem — Capcom — Survival Horror
A dual-protagonist setup balancing the series' warring impulses: terrifying first-person survival-horror sections starring new character Grace, and action-heavy Resident Evil 4-style sections with returning Leon S. Kennedy. An impressively scary, mournful atmosphere. "No PlayStation collection is complete without a great Resident Evil game."

## Returnal — Housemarque — Roguelite Third-Person Shooter
The first PS5 game (outside Astro's Playroom) to truly showcase the DualSense. Housemarque's refined arcade-shooter craft planted in a third-person roguelite that demands precision, aided by haptic triggers and immersive rumble. Despite uneven repetition, every PS5 owner should play it "to feel every bit of that $500 rumble."

## Marvel's Spider-Man 2 — Insomniac Games — Action-Adventure (superhero, PS5 exclusive)
"The word for Spider-Man 2 is confidence." An astonishingly slick superhero adventure combining the first game and Miles Morales, getting great mileage from bringing both heroes together. Peerless web-swinging, fluid combat, lightning-quick fast travel, ray-traced reflections — and Insomniac's boldest storytelling, taking on the Venom/symbiote saga.

## Street Fighter 6 — Capcom — Fighting
A textbook revival with deep rewarding combat, a series-best character lineup, and extravagant design — and a revolution in accessibility. The World Tour campaign teaches fundamentals, while Modern and Dynamic control schemes welcome all skill levels. "The first fighting game in a very long time that can claim to be for everyone."

## Tetris Effect: Connected — Enhance Games — Puzzle
Tetris reimagined by Tetsuya Mizuguchi (Rez, Lumines) as an almost spiritual, transcendental audiovisual experience that sweeps the player into a flow state, yoking block-matching to a metronomic techno beat. "A stunning, transporting reinterpretation of an all-time classic."
