## Top 3 Picks
- **Alien: Isolation (2014, Enhanced for PS5)** — Pure survival horror with a relentless, adaptive Xenomorph.
- **Alan Wake 2 (2023)** — Stylish psychological horror where story and atmosphere hit as hard as the monsters.
- **Dead Space Remake (2023)** — Brutal, claustrophobic sci-fi survival horror perfected for modern hardware.

---

## 1. Alien: Isolation — 10/10 — Survival Horror / Stealth (2014, Creative Assembly / SEGA)
Playtime 18–22h. An adaptive AI Xenomorph that learns, listens, and checks vents — slow-burn terror where you're hunted, not heroic. '70s sci-fi aesthetic; barely any tools (noisemakers, motion tracker). "The scariest stealth game ever made." Best PS5 horror game overall.

## 2. Alan Wake 2 — 9.8/10 — Psychological Horror / Third-Person Action (2023, Remedy)
Playtime 20–25h. Dual protagonists (FBI agent Saga Anderson and Alan Wake); reality-bending mechanics; heavy Twin Peaks energy. Limited resources, creepy shadows, jump scares — but the real power is atmosphere. Best reality-bending horror.

## 3. Dead Space Remake — 9.7/10 — Sci-Fi Survival Horror (2023, Motive / EA)
Playtime 15–20h. Full remake aboard the claustrophobic USG Ishimura; zero-G movement, 3D audio, body horror. Brutal third-person combat — aim for limbs with limited ammo. Nightmare sound design. Best sci-fi horror game on PS5.

## 4. Silent Hill 2 (Remake) — 9.6/10 — Survival / Psychological Horror (2024, Bloober Team / Konami)
Playtime ~10–12h. Ground-up remake; foggy streets and eerie silence turn guilt and grief into monsters. Tighter over-the-shoulder combat, limited ammo, fight-or-run choices; multiple endings; Akira Yamaoka soundtrack. "The blueprint for psychological horror done right."

## 5. Resident Evil 7: Biohazard — 9.4/10 — First-Person Survival Horror (2017, Capcom)
Playtime 9–11h. Returned the series to pure horror in a decaying Louisiana estate hunted by the Baker family. First-person shift makes horror personal; limited ammo, smart level design. RE Engine debut. Best pure survival horror.

## 6. Amnesia: The Bunker — 9.2/10 — First-Person Survival Horror (2023, Frictional Games)
Playtime ~4–6h. A WWI bunker with a photosensitive monster (the Beast), scarce ammo, a generator fuel timer, and randomized resources. Semi-open design, environmental traps, emergent tension. "Short games can bring long-lasting terror." Best compact terror experience.

## 7. Until Dawn — 9/10 — Interactive Horror / Narrative Adventure (2015, Supermassive Games)
Playtime 8–12h. A controllable horror movie: eight friends, branching paths, multiple endings, "butterfly effect" decisions. The closest to a **co-op** game on the list — pass the controller and have friends control different characters. Best for story-driven scares.

## 8. Returnal — 8.8/10 — Roguelike / Third-Person Shooter / Psychological Horror (2021, Housemarque)
Playtime 30–40h. A time loop on an alien planet; procedural levels, fast punishing combat, haunting sci-fi world, story told in fragments. "Existentially terrifying." Best for action-horror fans.

## 9. Visage — 8.7/10 — First-Person Psychological Survival Horror (2020, SadSquare Studio)
Playtime ~8–10h. A simple house becomes a living nightmare; no combat, light is your fragile lifeline. Silent Hill / P.T.-style tension. PS5 Enhanced Edition adds 4K/60fps, faster loads, DualSense haptics. Best psychological house-horror on PS5.

## 10. Resident Evil 4 Remake — 8.5/10 — Survival Horror / Third-Person Shooter (2023, Capcom)
Playtime 15–20h. Leon Kennedy's rescue op spirals into cult-and-parasite dread; balances action and anxiety with refined gunplay and resource management. Themes of identity and control. Best survival horror redefined.
